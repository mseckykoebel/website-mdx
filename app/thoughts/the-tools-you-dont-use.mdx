---
title: "The tools you don't use"
date: "2026-03-17"
---

There's a certain kind of productivity that looks like doing nothing. Deleting the app instead of configuring it. Saying no to the meeting instead of optimizing your calendar. Choosing the boring technology instead of the one with the better landing page. I think the most underrated skill in building things — software, companies, lives — is the willingness to leave a good tool on the shelf because you know it'll solve a problem you don't actually have yet. Restraint compounds just like effort does, but it's quieter about it.
